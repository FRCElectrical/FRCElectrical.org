# Educator's Handbook

## Using Your Robot as a Tool
* We write up most of our course with supplementing images. Getting across all of the elements of FRC electrical to your kids on an actual FRC robot may be easier, though, as this allows for a way to connect information together and allows for a better way for your kids to ask questions. Thus we recommend using a robot or a test bench for in person learning.
* Just one robot can be used to teach electrical theory, the control system, specific parts that your team uses, and mechanism wiring. We recommend you structure the way you teach your kids in a fashion similar to our course.  

### Project
* Have your kids make a diagram that has all of the core electronics + more, maybe on a sample robot. 
  * Rio/Systemcore
  * PDH
  * Battery
  * Radio
  * Motors

## Wire Planning
The cornerstone of preventable wiring errors must be refined.

### Project
* Work in tandem with your CAD/Design team to create prototype mechanisms for previous games
* Plan wiring using online software
  * Can be as simple as drawing wires on a picture 
  * Can be as complex as schematics on draw.io
* Enact the wiring as per your plan on the subsystem and determine where the plan failed
* Reference mechanism wiring in the mechanism wiring section


## What to do as a Lead/Mentor
Being a lead can sometimes be challenging especially in regards to teaching. It may at times be easier to do things all by yourself. 

But you should always include and inspire the kids to initiate.
